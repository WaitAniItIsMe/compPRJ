    A healthier life
    a happier life

    Leading a healthy life involves taking care of your physical, mental, and emotional well-being. One can do that with a proper and balanced diet, exercising regularly, staying hydrated and most importantly, getting jusst enough sleep.

    The importance of sleep

    Restoration: During sleep, our bodies repair and regenerate tissues, strengthen our immune system, and produce hormones that help regulate growth and appetite.
    Physical performance: Sleep is critical for athletic performance and recovery. It helps the body to repair and rebuild muscles and reduces the risk of injury.
    Disease prevention: Chronic sleep deprivation has been linked to a range of health problems, including obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and depression. Getting enough sleep can help prevent these conditions.

    Tips to a better night's sleep

    The quality of one's sleep is just as imprtant as how much they sleep. Here are some tips to a better night's sleep.

    fixed sleep schedule

    Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends.

    Limit Screen Time

    The blue light emitted by electronic devices can interfere with sleep, so it's best to avoid using them for at least an hour before bed.

    Avoid large meals

    Have a nice and light, as large heavy dinners can lead to trouble sleeping.





    Sleep quantity

    Infants (4-12 months)

    Infants need 12-16 hours of sleep per day, including naps.

    Toddlers (1-2 years)

    Toddlers need 11-14 hours of sleep per day, including naps.

    Preschoolers (3-5 years)

    Preschoolers need 10-13 hours of sleep per day, including naps.

    School-age children (6-12 years)

    School-age children need 9-12 hours of sleep per day.

    Adolescents (13-18 years)

    Adolescents need 8-10 hours of sleep per day.
